{# ===== 01 · BACKGROUND ===== #}
01 · BACKGROUND

The operational layer is where strategy meets reality. I spent years there before writing systems for it.

Three decades inside operational environments — leading teams, owning processes, watching workflows break and rebuild themselves under real load. That perspective shapes everything I now build.

{# Pulled quote — thesis of the page, lifted out of the narrative for weight. #}

Most operational software is built by people who have read about workflows. I built mine after running them.

The shift from running operations to building systems for them was gradual, then sudden. AI-assisted development was the inflection point — the difference between "I wish this existed" and "I'm shipping it on Saturday."
